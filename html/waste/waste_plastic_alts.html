The Single-Use Problem

Single-use plastics are used for minutes but last for centuries. They break down into microplastics, polluting our oceans and entering the food chain. We can reduce demand by choosing durable, reusable alternatives.

Biodegradable vs. Compostable

Be careful with labels! "Biodegradable" plastic simply breaks down into smaller plastic pieces. Look for "Certified Compostable" items, which actually turn into organic soil matter under the right conditions.
