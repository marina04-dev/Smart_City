Turning "Trash" into "Black Gold"

Composting is nature's way of recycling. It transforms organic food waste into a nutrient-rich soil additive that can fertilize gardens, houseplants, and parks. Crucially, it diverts food waste from landfills where it would otherwise generate methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

The Perfect Recipe: Greens and Browns

A healthy compost pile needs a balance of two types of materials:

  • Greens (Nitrogen): Vegetable scraps, fruit peels, coffee grounds, fresh grass clippings.
  • Browns (Carbon): Dry leaves, cardboard, shredded paper, straw, sawdust.

Tip: Aim for a ratio of 2 parts Brown to 1 part Green to prevent odors.

⛔ What NOT to Compost

To avoid attracting pests and creating bad smells, never add:

  • Meat, fish, or bones
  • Dairy products (cheese, milk)
  • Oils or greasy foods
  • Diseased plants
  • Pet waste (dogs/cats)

Benefits of Composting

By composting at home, you can reduce your household waste by up to 30%. Plus, you get free, high-quality fertilizer that improves soil structure and helps retain moisture in your garden.
