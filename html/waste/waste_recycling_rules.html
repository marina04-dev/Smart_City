The Reality of Modern Recycling

Recycling is one of the easiest ways to help the environment, but it is often misunderstood. Not all plastic is recyclable, and putting the wrong item in the bin (known as "wish-cycling") can contaminate an entire truckload, forcing it all to go to the landfill.

The Golden Rules of Recycling

  • Clean it: Rinse out all food residue. Greasy pizza boxes cannot be recycled!
  • Dry it: Wet paper fibers are often rejected by sorting machines.
  • Keep it Loose: Never bag your recyclables in a plastic bag; throw them in the bin loose.

Know Your Codes: A Quick Guide

Plastic items are stamped with a resin ID code from 1 to 7. Here is what they mean:

  • #1 (PET) & #2 (HDPE): Water bottles, milk jugs, detergent containers. Widely Recyclable.
  • #3 (PVC) & #4 (LDPE): Pipes, squeezy bottles, plastic bags. Check Local Rules.
  • #5 (PP): Yoghurt pots, margarine tubs. Increasingly Recyclable.
  • #6 (PS): Styrofoam, takeaway cups. Almost Never Recyclable.
  • #7 (Other): Mixed plastics. Difficult to Recycle.
