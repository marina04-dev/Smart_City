Understanding home insulation

Home insulation plays a critical role in maintaining a comfortable indoor temperature by reducing heat transfer between the inside and outside of your house. Proper insulation keeps your home warm in winter and cool in summer, while also reducing energy consumption.

Did you know? Poor insulation can account for up to 30% of heat loss in an average home.

Types of Home Insulation

There are several insulation options available, each suited for different applications:

  • Fiberglass Insulation: Affordable and commonly used in walls and attics.
  • Spray Foam Insulation: Excellent air sealing and high R-value.
  • Cellulose Insulation: Eco-friendly and made from recycled materials.
  • Rigid Foam Boards: Ideal for foundations and exterior walls.
  • Mineral Wool: Fire-resistant and great for soundproofing.

Benefits of Proper Insulation

  • Lower heating and cooling costs
  • Improved indoor comfort
  • Reduced environmental impact
  • Better sound control
  • Increased home value

Key Zones to Check

  • Attic: Heat rises. A poorly insulated attic is the #1 source of energy loss.
  • Windows: Double-glazing and weatherstripping prevent drafts.
  • Doors: Use a "draft snake" to stop air leaking under doors.
