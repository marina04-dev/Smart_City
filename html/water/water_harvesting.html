Why Let it Drain Away?

Rainwater harvesting is the collection and storage of rain, rather than allowing it to run off. Just 1 inch of rain on a 1,000 sq ft roof can yield over 600 gallons of water.

Anatomy of a Rain Barrel

  • Catchment Area: Your roof surface where rain lands.
  • Conveyance: Gutters and downspouts that move the water.
  • Storage: Rain barrels (50 gallons) or larger cisterns.
  • Filtration: A mesh screen to keep out leaves and mosquitoes.

Safety First: Non-Potable Water

Warning: Unless you have a professional purification system, do not drink harvested rainwater. It may contain bacteria from bird droppings or chemicals from roofing materials. Use it for watering plants, washing cars, or flushing toilets.

Benefits

Using rainwater reduces demand on the municipal supply, lowers your water bill, and plants actually prefer it because it's free of chlorine and fluoride.
