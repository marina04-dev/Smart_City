What is Xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping is a landscaping method designed to minimize the need for irrigation. It involves choosing drought-resistant native plants (like succulents, lavender, or ornamental grasses) that thrive in your local climate without extra water.

The Magic of Mulch

Adding a layer of mulch (wood chips, bark, or compost) around your plants acts like a blanket. It prevents water evaporation, suppresses weeds, and keeps the soil cool. Result: You water 50% less often.

Smart Watering Rules

  • Timing: Water early in the morning (before 10 AM) or late evening to prevent evaporation.
  • Technique: Water the roots, not the leaves. Wet leaves can lead to fungal diseases.
  • Frequency: Deep, infrequent watering encourages roots to grow deep and strong.

Drip Irrigation

Drip systems deliver water directly to the base of the plant drop-by-drop. They are 90% efficient, unlike sprinklers which often spray water onto sidewalks and driveways.
