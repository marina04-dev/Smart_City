The New Normal?

Climate change is making weather patterns more extreme. Many regions are experiencing longer, hotter summers and less predictable rainfall. "Water Scarcity" is becoming a global reality, making conservation a necessity, not just a hobby.

The Impact of Scarcity

  • Agriculture: Crops fail, leading to higher food prices.
  • Environment: Rivers dry up, harming fish and wildlife habitats.
  • Energy: Hydroelectric dams produce less power when water levels drop.

What You Can Do (Emergency Tips)

1. Stop the Leaks: A dripping tap wastes 5,000 liters a year. Fix it immediately.

2. Turn it Off: Don't let the water run while brushing your teeth or shaving.

3. Full Loads Only: Only run your dishwasher and washing machine when they are completely full.

Greywater Recycling

During extreme drought, you can collect "Greywater" (rinse water from dishwashing or showers) and use it to flush toilets or water ornamental plants. It's a second life for every drop.
