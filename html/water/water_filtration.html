Why Filter Your Water?

While municipal water is generally safe, it can contain chlorine, sediment, and microplastics. Home filtration improves taste, removes contaminants, and eliminates the need for expensive, polluting bottled water.

Common Filtration Methods

  • Activated Carbon: The most common type (pitchers, fridge filters). Removes chlorine and bad tastes.
  • Reverse Osmosis (RO): Removes 99% of contaminants, including lead and arsenic. Requires installation under the sink.
  • UV Purification: Uses ultraviolet light to kill bacteria and viruses. Often used in rural areas.

Bottled Water vs. Filtration

Cost: Drinking bottled water costs ~2,000x more than tap water.
Waste: Millions of plastic bottles end up in oceans every day. A simple $20 filter pitcher can replace 300 plastic bottles.

Maintenance Is Key

A filter only works if you change it! An old, clogged filter can actually grow bacteria. Set a reminder on your phone to change your cartridge every 2-6 months depending on the model.
