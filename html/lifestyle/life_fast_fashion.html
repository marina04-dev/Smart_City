What is Fast Fashion?

The fashion industry produces 10% of global carbon emissions—more than all international flights combined. "Fast Fashion" encourages a disposable culture where clothes are made cheaply, worn once, and trashed.

The Hidden Environmental Costs

  • Water Pollution: Textile dyeing is the second largest polluter of water globally.
  • Microplastics: Synthetic fabrics like Polyester shed plastic fibers in the wash that end up in the ocean.
  • Landfills: The average consumer throws away 70 lbs (32kg) of clothing per year.

The "30 Wears" Test

Before buying a new item, ask yourself: "Will I wear this at least 30 times?" If the answer is no, put it back. This simple rule stops impulse buying and reduces waste.

Solutions: Build a Conscious Wardrobe

1. Thrift & Swap: Buying second-hand gives clothes a new life without using new resources.
2. Choose Natural Fibers: Opt for Organic Cotton, Linen, or Hemp instead of plastic-based Polyester.
3. Repair: Learn to sew a button or fix a hem. Loved clothes last longer.
