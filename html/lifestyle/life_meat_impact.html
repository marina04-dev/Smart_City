What's on Your Plate Matters

Food production accounts for 26% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Animal agriculture, particularly beef and lamb, has a disproportionately high impact compared to plant-based foods.

The Numbers: Beef vs. Beans

Producing 100 grams of protein creates:

  • Beef: 50 kg of CO2 equivalents.
  • Cheese: 11 kg of CO2 equivalents.
  • Poultry: 5.7 kg of CO2 equivalents.
  • Beans/Peas: Only 0.8 kg of CO2 equivalents!

The Water Footprint

It takes approximately 1,800 gallons of water to produce just one pound of beef. In comparison, lentils and tofu require a fraction of that water, making them a drought-friendly choice.

Try "Meatless Mondays"

You don't have to go fully vegan to make a difference. The "Flexitarian" approach encourages reducing meat rather than eliminating it. Skipping meat just one day a week saves thousands of gallons of water and reduces your carbon footprint significantly.
